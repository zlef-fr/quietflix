Privacy Policy

QuietFlix browser extension · Last updated 14 June 2026

In short: QuietFlix collects nothing, stores nothing about you, and sends nothing anywhere. There are no accounts, no analytics, and no network requests. It only adjusts the volume of video elements on Netflix, entirely on your own device.

What data we collect

None. QuietFlix does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal or usage data. It has no servers, no accounts, no telemetry, and no third-party SDKs.

What is stored, and where

The only thing QuietFlix saves is your local settings, kept in your browser via the chrome.storage.local API:

  • Whether the extension is on or off
  • Your preview-volume offset (e.g. previews at 70% of player volume)
  • The "mute previews entirely" toggle
  • The most recent volume QuietFlix detected in the Netflix player, used as the target level

This data never leaves your device. It is not synced to us in any way and is removed if you uninstall the extension.

Permissions we request, and why

  • storage — to remember the local settings listed above.
  • *://*.netflix.com/* (host access) — so the extension can read and set the volume of <video> elements on Netflix pages. QuietFlix runs on no other websites.

Network activity

QuietFlix makes no network requests of its own. It contains no remote code; all logic ships inside the extension package and runs locally.

Children's privacy

QuietFlix does not collect data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new date.
