Under the hood

How it works

A single tiny content script — no remote code, no accounts, no network calls.

1

Reads your player

When you set volume in the real Netflix player, QuietFlix captures that level as the source of truth.

2

Levels every preview

On browse, title and search pages it clamps each preview's audio to match — optionally a touch quieter.

3

Survives Netflix

A MutationObserver + watchdog catch lazy-loaded previews and SPA navigation, re-applying instantly.

4

Stays out of the way

No UI unless you want it. A minimal popup offers an offset slider and a full-mute toggle.

Questions

Good to know

Does it collect any data?

No. There are no accounts, no analytics, and no network requests. The only permission it holds is storage (to remember your settings) and host access to netflix.com. Nothing ever leaves your browser.

Will it slow down Netflix?

No. It's a single small content script that only touches <video> elements on Netflix. There's no measurable performance footprint.

The previews aren't matching — what do I do?

Press play in the real player once so QuietFlix can capture your actual volume, then open the popup and hit Re-sync now. If Netflix changed something under the hood, the watchdog usually recovers on its own within a second.

Can I make previews quieter than my shows?

Yes. Use the preview offset slider in the popup — e.g. set previews to 70% of your player volume. Or flip Mute previews entirely for total silence while keeping the visuals.

Why isn't it on the Chrome Web Store?

It's a single-purpose free utility shipped unpacked for now. Loading unpacked takes about a minute (see Install) and keeps it fully transparent — you can read every line of the source in the zip.
